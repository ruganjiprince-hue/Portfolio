Renewable Energy Grid Demand & Generation Analytics

A time-series analytics project exploring electricity demand patterns, renewable generation dynamics, and wind energy penetration using high-resolution grid data from Ireland and Northern Ireland.

Python Pandas Time-Series Analysis Statistical Testing Data Visualization Autocorrelation (ACF)

Problem Context

Modern electricity grids must balance fluctuating demand with increasing renewable energy integration. Understanding seasonal patterns, daily cycles, and renewable penetration is critical for ensuring grid stability and efficient energy planning.

Time-Series Demand & Generation Analysis

Using 15-minute resolution grid data, daily and weekly averages were computed to reveal cyclic consumption behavior. Clear weekday-weekend demand differences were observed, reflecting human activity patterns. Renewable generation was closely aligned with demand, demonstrating effective grid responsiveness.

Key Analytical Insights

  • Strong seasonal variation in electricity demand
  • Solar generation peaks in summer months
  • Wind contributes approximately 30–33% of total demand
  • Over-generation events indicate need for storage flexibility
  • Statistically significant demand differences between weekdays and weekends

Statistical Testing & Autocorrelation

Welch’s t-test was applied to compare weekday and weekend demand, confirming statistically significant differences in consumption patterns. Autocorrelation analysis (ACF) was performed on wind and solar generation to evaluate periodicity and predictability characteristics.

Solar generation exhibited strong daily periodic behavior, while wind generation displayed more stochastic characteristics, highlighting forecasting challenges in renewable energy systems.

Wind Energy Penetration Analysis

Wind penetration rate was calculated as the ratio of wind generation to total electricity demand. The results demonstrate substantial renewable contribution while also revealing periods of over-generation, emphasizing the importance of grid flexibility and storage solutions.

Impact & Relevance

This project demonstrates how time-series analytics and statistical modeling can provide actionable insights for renewable energy integration. The findings support data-driven energy planning, sustainable infrastructure development, and improved grid reliability.

It highlights the importance of combining statistical rigor with practical interpretation when analyzing complex real-world systems.
