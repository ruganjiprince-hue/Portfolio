Global Health and Life Expectancy Prediction

A machine learning and data analytics project exploring how economic, healthcare, and demographic indicators influence global life expectancy, with a focus on interpretability and model performance comparison.

Python Pandas Scikit-learn Feature Engineering Regression Models Model Evaluation

Problem Context

Life expectancy is one of the most important indicators of population health and socioeconomic development. The objective of this project was to identify the most influential factors affecting life expectancy and to build predictive models capable of capturing complex relationships between health, economic, and demographic variables.

Exploratory Data Analysis

The dataset spans multiple countries over several years and includes indicators such as GDP, adult mortality, infant deaths, BMI, schooling, and vaccination coverage. Exploratory analysis revealed persistent disparities between developed and developing countries, with noticeable gaps in life expectancy progression.

Data Cleaning and Feature Engineering

  • Handled missing values using statistical and KNN-based imputation
  • Analyzed and mitigated outliers in GDP and expenditure features
  • Engineered composite features such as Infant Survival Rate
  • Created indices combining education and income variables

Key Modeling Insights

  • Ensemble models significantly outperformed linear baselines
  • Random Forest achieved the strongest overall predictive performance
  • Top predictors included Income Composition, Adult Mortality, and Infant Deaths
  • Reduced-feature models maintained competitive accuracy while improving interpretability

Modeling and Evaluation

Multiple regression models were evaluated, including Decision Trees, Random Forest, K-Nearest Neighbors, and Gradient Boosting. Hyperparameter tuning was conducted using GridSearchCV, and performance was compared using RMSE, MAE, and R² metrics.

Impact and Interpretation

The findings confirm that life expectancy is shaped by a combination of healthcare access, economic stability, and demographic conditions. By identifying the most influential features, this project supports evidence-based policy discussions and highlights actionable areas for improving global health outcomes.

The project demonstrates the importance of combining predictive accuracy with interpretability to ensure that data-driven insights remain meaningful and socially responsible.
